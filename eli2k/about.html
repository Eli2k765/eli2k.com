About this Site

Last updated: May 4th, 2023

Why I made the Site

Over the past year, I've been compiling extensive notes on a range of subjects, with a particular focus on cybersecurity. Originally, I planned to create a GitBook to share them but decided to take a more involved and independent approach. I had a server and domain that I mainly used for email and a few other services, and adding an Nginx server was relatively easy since it runs on NixOS. I designed the site with inspiration from Sadgrl, which was featured in an article discussing the differences between the "old web" and the "new web." Although the article is no longer available on Wayback Machine, the Sadgrl website is still active.

What to Expect

The content on my website consists mostly of slightly revised pages from my notebook, which I used to study for certifications such as OSCP, as well as topics that I found interesting. Since enrolling in WGUs cybersecurity and information assurance program, I plan to share all the notes I take for each course on the website. Currently, there is no way for users to provide feedback or chat with me, but I hope to implement those features in the future. If you'd like to chat, feel free to email me at erodgers@eli2k.com.
