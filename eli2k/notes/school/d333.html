D333 - Ethics in Tech


Last updated: May 4th, 2023

Unit 1

What are ethics?

  • Ethics is a code of behavior defined by the group to which an individual belongs.
  • Morals are personal principles that guide individual decisions about right and wrong.
  • Acting with integrity means adhering to a personal code of principles.

The relationship between ethics, laws, and codes of ethics:

  • Laws are systems of rules that define permissible and prohibited actions, enforced by institutions.
  • Codes of ethics establish principles and core values essential to one's work.
  • Legal activities may not necessarily be ethical.

Trends increasing the likelihood of unethical behavior:

  • Globalization has made it challenging to consistently apply ethical principles.
  • Economic pressures may lead organizations to resort to unethical behavior.
  • Powerful individuals may act immorally due to their aggressive nature, privileged access, and inflated self-belief.

Corporate social responsibility (CSR) and the importance of fostering good business ethics:

  • CSR emphasizes an organization's ethical responsibility toward stakeholders and the environment.
  • Supply chain sustainability is a component of CSR focused on meeting present needs without compromising future generations.
  • Pursuing CSR goals fosters goodwill, consistent operations, good business practices, legal protection, and positive publicity.

Measures for improving business ethics:

  • Appointing a corporate ethics officer.
  • Setting and modeling high ethical standards by the board of directors.
  • Establishing a corporate code of ethics.
  • Conducting social audits.
  • Providing ethics training for employees.
  • Including ethical criteria in employee appraisals.
  • Creating an ethical work environment.

Including ethical considerations in decision-making:

  • Define the problem.
  • Identify alternatives.
  • Choose an alternative.
  • Implement the decision.
  • Monitor the results.
  • Incorporate ethical considerations by involving stakeholders, considering applicable laws and guidelines, and assessing the decision's impact.

Trends increasing the risk of unethical use of information technology:

  • The growth of the internet, social networks, and reliance on information systems.
  • Increased ability to capture, store, and analyze vast amounts of personal data.
  • Inadequate emphasis on ethics and human values amidst technological advancements.

Unit 2

Government Surveillance and Privacy Laws

  • CALEA requires the telecommunications industry to build tools into its products that federal investigators can use—after gaining a court order—to eavesdrop on conversations and intercept electronic communications.
  • The USA PATRIOT Act modified 15 existing statutes and gave sweeping new powers both to domestic law enforcement and to international intelligence agencies.
  • The Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act Amendments Act of 2004 authorized intelligence gathering on individuals not affiliated with any known terrorist organization.
  • The Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act of 1978 Amendments Act of 2008 granted the NSA expanded authority to collect international communications without court-approved warrants.
  • The PATRIOT Sunsets Extension Act granted a four-year extension of provisions of the USA PATRIOT Act.
  • The USA Freedom Act terminated the bulk collection of telephone metadata by the NSA and restored authorization for roving wiretaps and tracking of lone wolf terrorists.

Data Privacy and Protection

  • "Fair information practices" is a term for a set of guidelines that govern the collection and use of personal data.
  • The OECD for the Protection of Privacy and Transborder Data Flows of Personal Data created a set of fair information practices.
  • The European Union Data Protection Directive requires member countries to ensure data transferred to non-EU countries is protected.
  • The European–United States Privacy Shield Data Transfer Program Guidelines is a stopgap measure that allows businesses to transfer personal data about European citizens to the United States.
  • The GDPR takes effect on May 2018 and addresses the export of personal data outside the EU.
  • FOIA grants citizens the right to access certain information and records of the federal government upon request.
  • The Privacy Act prohibits U.S. government agencies from concealing the existence of any personal data record-keeping system.

Consumer Profiling and Ethical Issues

  • Companies use many different methods to collect personal data about visitors to their websites.
  • Consumer data privacy has become a major marketing issue.
  • A data breach is the unintended release of sensitive data or the access of sensitive data by unauthorized individuals.

E-Discovery

  • Discovery is part of the pretrial phase of a lawsuit.
  • E-discovery is the collection, preparation, review, and production of electronically stored information for use in criminal and civil actions and proceedings.
  • Predictive coding is a process that couples human intelligence with computer-driven concept searching.

Workplace Monitoring

  • Many organizations have developed IT usage policies to protect against employee abuses.
  • About 80% of U.S. firms record and review employee communications and activities on the job.
  • The use of fitness trackers in the workplace has opened up potential new legal and ethical issues.

Advanced Surveillance Technologies and Ethical Issues

  • Surveillance cameras are used in major cities around the world.
  • An EDR records a host of data about a vehicle and can be used as evidence.
  • Stalking apps are an extreme example of apps that can be used to track and record personal data.

Computer Incidents and Effects

  • The number of security incidents reported annually continues to rise.
  • Exploits are a type of malicious code that take advantage of known vulnerabilities.
  • Various individuals launch computer attacks with different motivations.
  • The Computer Fraud and Abuse Act is a key U.S. statute that addresses computer-related crimes.

Social Networks and Social Media

  • Social media has become a common way for people to interact, share information, and build communities online.
  • Businesses use social networks for various purposes, including advertising, customer service, and sales.
  • Social media marketing involves targeting specific demographics and utilizes organic, paid, and earned media.
  • Individuals use social networks for personal communication and job searching.

Ethical Issues in Social Networks and Social Media

  • Cyberabuse, cyberharassment, and cyberstalking are serious risks and sources of distress.
  • Privacy concerns can arise from inappropriate material, terms of use agreements, and employee monitoring.
  • Organizations can address legal issues and guide user behavior through social media policies and awareness programs.

Implementing Strong Security Programs

  • IT security practices focus on maintaining the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information.
  • Organizations need to develop a risk-based security strategy, disaster recovery plan, security policies, and conduct regular audits.
  • Reasonable assurance is a security concept that balances the cost of security controls against the benefit gained.
  • Authentication methods, encryption, intrusion detection systems, and employee training are all components of a strong security program.

Unit 3

Data Accuracy in Information Technology

  • Data accuracy is associated with data legitimacy, while data privacy is aligned with information access and consent.
  • Data accuracy and privacy must be safeguarded to protect the general public and businesses.
  • The First Amendment protects freedom of speech, but certain types of speech, such as perjury, fraud, defamation, and obscenity, are not protected.
  • Existing laws protect personal data accuracy to varying degrees.

Safeguarding Data Accuracy and Regulations

  • Laws and regulations are struggling to keep up with the speed and complexity of technological advancements.
  • The CIA Security Triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability) is crucial in preserving data accuracy.
  • The Fair Credit Reporting Act ensures accuracy, fairness, and privacy in credit information.
  • The EU Data Protection Directive requires member countries to protect data transferred to non-EU countries.

Impact of Invalid Data in Information Technology

  • Data accuracy benefits human understanding and efficiency.
  • Predictive coding aids data accuracy by combining computer-driven processes with human guidance.
  • Data integrity guarantees valid and accurate data through measures and processes.

Impact of Inaccurately Captured Data

  • Inaccurate health care data can result from human error, IT system issues, or poorly designed software systems.
  • Human error is the most common cause of data inaccuracy.
  • Posting information on social media can have both positive and negative impacts.
  • Machine learning systems can learn from incomplete or occasionally inaccurate data inputs without severe consequences.

Impact of Manipulated or Misrepresented Data

  • Data manipulation organizes and presents data for easier understanding, while misrepresentation deliberately influences outcomes through false or misleading data.
  • Misrepresented data can result from ignorance, laziness, or deliberate deception.
  • Social media presence can impact employment and privacy.
  • Facial recognition technology raises concerns about anonymity and privacy.

Information Technology Threats: Political and Government Control

  • Technological developments are outpacing laws, raising ethical concerns.
  • Big data and predictive analytics are reshaping power dynamics and political landscapes.
  • Crowdsourced data provides sophisticated and accurate information.
  • Misrepresenting information can have significant economic and societal consequences.

Threats Involving Misrepresenting Information in Targeted Agendas

  • Data quality issues and inadequate design can harm data practices.
  • Deregulation and financial derivatives contributed to the 2008 global financial crisis.
  • Misrepresentation of information includes intentional nondisclosure and false reporting.
  • The Sarbanes-Oxley Act was enacted to improve corporate disclosures after financial scandals.

Threats Involving Fake News Releases

  • The First Amendment doesn't protect data inaccuracies or speech promoting hate, defamation, obscenities, pornography, or sedition.
  • Fake news spreads deliberate misinformation to influence people's views.
  • The internet's unregulated nature and advertising-driven incentives contribute to the proliferation of fake news.

Unit 4

What does the term intellectual property encompass, and what measures can organizations take to protect their intellectual property?

  • Intellectual property includes works of the mind owned or created by individuals or groups, such as art, books, inventions, music, and processes.
  • Copyrights, patents, trademarks, and trade secrets are forms of intellectual property protection.
  • Copyright grants exclusive rights to distribute, display, perform, or reproduce an original work.
  • Copyright infringement occurs when someone copies a substantial part of a copyrighted work without permission.
  • Patents grant the owner the right to exclude others from making, using, or selling a protected invention.
  • Trade secrets require information to have economic value and be kept secret by the owner.
  • Organizations can use nondisclosure clauses, noncompete agreements, and employment contracts to protect trade secrets.

What are some of the current issues associated with the protection of intellectual property?

  • Plagiarism involves stealing someone's ideas or words and presenting them as one's own.
  • Reverse engineering is the process of breaking something down to understand, copy, or improve it.
  • Open source code allows for collaboration and improvement, but proper licensing and attribution are important.
  • Competitive intelligence is legally obtained information used to gain a competitive advantage.
  • Trademarks differentiate one company's products from another's.
  • Cybersquatting involves registering domain names for famous trademarks with the intention of selling them at a high price.

What are some effective strategies for developing quality systems?

  • Software development methodologies provide a standard work process for developing high-quality software.
  • ISO 9000 series standards require organizations to develop formal quality management systems.
  • ISO 9001 certification is often required by businesses and government agencies for contract eligibility.

Unit 5

Protection of Freedom of Expression

  • The First Amendment protects Americans' rights to freedom of religion, expression, and assembly, including the right to speak anonymously.
  • Types of expressions not protected: obscene speech, defamation, incitement of panic or crime, "fighting words," and sedition.

Federal Laws Affecting Online Freedom of Expression

  • Communications Decency Act (CDA): Aims to protect children from online pornography and provides immunity to ISPs that publish user-generated content.
  • Child Online Protection Act (COPA): Prohibits making harmful material available to minors online.
  • Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA): Addresses copyright-related issues and limits ISP liability for copyright infringement.

Freedom of Expression Issues Related to Information Technology

  • Internet censorship: Control or suppression of information publishing or accessing on the internet.
  • SLAPP lawsuits: Strategic lawsuits against public participation aimed at silencing critics.
  • Anonymity: Importance of expressing opinions without revealing identity, especially in countries with limited free speech.
  • Doxing: Obtaining and posting someone's private personal information without permission.
  • Monitoring of online opinions by businesses and protection of confidential information.
  • John Doe lawsuits: Efforts to reveal the identity of anonymous internet users causing harm to organizations.
  • Legal protection of annoying, critical, demeaning, or offensive speech under the First Amendment.
  • Voluntary restrictions by ISPs and social networking sites on hate speech without violating First Amendment rights.
  • Considerations and precautions regarding pornography in the workplace to avoid harassment lawsuits.
  • Sexting, CAN-SPAM Act, and fictionalized news accounts as additional freedom of expression issues.

Relationship between IT Investment and Productivity Growth

  • Gross domestic product (GDP) per capita as a measure of the material standard of living.
  • Labor productivity: Efficiency comparison between goods/services produced and labor hours used.
  • IT investments enable innovation and contribute to productivity growth.
  • Quantifying specific impacts of IT investments on productivity is complex due to various factors.

Impact of AI, Machine Learning, Robotics, and NLP on the Future Workforce

  • Advancements in AI, machine learning, robotics, and natural language processing will transform work tasks and roles.
  • Approximately 45% of human work activities have automation potential.
  • Full realization of automation potential may take decades.
  • AI simulates human intelligence, machine learning improves performance with experience, robotics performs precise or hazardous tasks, and NLP enables understanding of natural languages.

Impact of IT Application on Healthcare

  • Healthcare costs in the United States are expected to increase.
  • Electronic medical records (EMR) and electronic health records (EHR) improve information management and sharing.
  • Health information exchange (HIE) facilitates data sharing between healthcare organizations.
  • Personal health records (PHR) enable individuals to control their health information.
  • Clinical decision support (CDS), computerized provider order entry (CPOE), telehealth, and telemedicine enhance healthcare delivery and access.

Unit 6

Relationships and Ethical Issues for IT Workers:

  • Relationship with Employers:
    • Setting and enforcing ethical IT use policies
    • Whistle-blowing and protection of trade secrets
  • Relationship with Clients:
    • Defining responsibilities and fulfilling project requirements
    • Objectivity, conflict of interest, fraud, misrepresentation, breach of contract
  • Relationship with Suppliers:
    • Developing ethical working relationships
    • Avoiding actions that can be perceived as unethical
  • Relationship with Other Professionals:
    • Mentoring colleagues, demonstrating loyalty
    • Avoiding résumé inflation, inappropriate sharing of information
  • Relationship with IT Users:
    • Addressing software piracy and inappropriate use of IT resources
    • Ensuring proper information sharing and privacy protection
  • Relationship with Society:
    • Practicing IT in a way that benefits society and avoids harm

Encouraging IT Worker Professionalism:

  • Subscribing to a professional code of ethics
  • Joining and participating in professional organizations
  • Obtaining relevant certifications
  • Supporting government licensing where available

Ethical Issues for IT Users and Encouraging Ethical Behavior:

  • Software piracy, inappropriate resource use, and information sharing
  • Establishing guidelines, defining acceptable use policies (AUP)
  • Implementing security measures, ensuring compliance with laws and policies

Legal and Ethical Issues with Contingent Workers, H-1B Visa Holders, and Offshore Outsourcing:

  • Classification, treatment, and pay of contingent workers
  • Hiring H-1B visa holders and impacts on job opportunities and wages
  • Ethical considerations in outsourcing and offshore outsourcing

Whistle-blowing and Ethical Issues:

  • Definition and purpose of whistle-blowing
  • Ethical implications, loyalty to employer, potential consequences
  • Steps in an effective whistle-blowing process

Green Computing and Supporting Initiatives:

  • Concerns for environmentally responsible IT practices
  • Goals of reducing hazardous materials, lowering costs, and safe disposal
  • EPEAT system, Restriction of Hazardous Substances Directive
