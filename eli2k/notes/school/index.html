WGU Class Summaries

An overview of how I approached classes in the Cybersecurity and Information Assurance program at WGU. O represents classes that have an Objective assessment and P represents classes that have a Performance assessment.

Each class has a link to the notes I took for that class and a brief summary on my thoughts. Generally, my approach was to do the Course Planning Guide for every class, any section that I performed poorly in I would look up recommended study material on the WGU subreddit. After glancing over whatever resources I could find, and before taking any notes, I would take the pre-assessment if it was available, if not I would find a similar test online to evaluate my knowledge level. Then, I would take notes on anything I did poorly on or was just curious about before taking the final.

Completed

  • Introduction to IT - C182
    Students are presented with various IT disciplines including systems and services, network and security, scripting and programming, data management, and business of IT, with a survey of technologies in every area and how they relate to each other and to the business.
  • Critical Thinking and Logic - C168
    The goal of this course is to encourage techniques that increase knowledge and application of a systematic process for exploring issues that take you beyond an unexamined point of view.
  • Integrated Physical Sciences - C165
    This course provides students with an overview of the basic principles and unifying ideas of the physical sciences: physics, chemistry, and earth sciences.
  • Introduction to Geography - C255
    This course is an introduction to the study of geography, including physical and human geography, cartography, remote sensing, and GIS, as well as the geography of regions, population, and resources.
  • American Politics and the US Constitution - C963
    This course is an introduction to the principles and practices of American government and politics, including the Constitution, federalism, civil rights, and public policy.
  • IT Foundations (A+ Core 1) - C393
    This course is a foundational course in IT that covers basic concepts such as computer hardware and software, operating systems, computer networks, databases, and programming concepts.
  • IT Applications (A+ Core 2) - C394
    This class covers the basic concepts and principles of information technology and its applications. The class covers topics such as hardware, software, and data management, and how to apply these concepts to real-world problems and solutions.
  • Business of IT - Applications (ITILv4) - C846
    This class focuses on the business side of information technology, including the use of technology to improve business operations, and the impact of technology on organizational strategy and performance. It covers topics such as IT project management, IT service management, IT governance, and IT service delivery.
  • Legal Issues in Information Security - C841
    This class covers legal issues related to information security, including laws and regulations governing data protection, intellectual property, and cybercrime. It covers topics such as data privacy, cybercrime, and digital copyright laws and their impact on the use of technology.
  • Web Development Foundations - C779
    This class covers the basics of web development, including web design, coding, and web development tools. It covers topics such as HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and web development frameworks.
  • Business of IT - Project Management (Project+) - C176
    This class covers the principles of project management in the context of information technology, including project planning, execution, and control. It covers topics such as project scheduling, resource allocation, and risk management.
  • Networks - C480
    This class is designed to help students understand the principles of computer networks and how data is transmitted over them. Students will learn about the different types of networks, such as LAN, WAN, and wireless networks, and the protocols that are used to transmit data over them. They will also learn about network security and troubleshooting.
  • Applied Probability and Statistics - C955
    This class covers the principles of applied probability and statistics, including probability distributions, statistical inference, and statistical methods.
  • Scripting and Programming - Foundations - C173
    This class is an introduction to programming and the fundamentals of computer science. Students will learn the basics of programming concepts such as data types, control structures, and algorithms.
  • English Composition I - C455
    Students will be introduced to different types of writing such as argumentative, descriptive, and expository writing. They will learn how to research, organize and write effective essays, and use proper grammar, punctuation, and citation techniques.
  • Introduction to Cryptography - C839
    Introduction to Cryptography provides students with knowledge of cryptographic algorithms, protocols, and their uses in the protection of information in various states.
  • Data Management - Foundations - C175
    Provides an introduction to the principles of data management and its practical applications. The course covers topics such as data modeling, data warehousing, data governance, and data quality. Students will learn how to design and implement data management solutions, as well as how to analyze and interpret data.
  • Data Management - Applications - C170
    Builds on the concepts covered in Data Management - Foundations. It focuses on the practical applications of data management, including data warehousing, data mining, and business intelligence. Students will learn how to use data management tools and techniques to solve real-world problems, as well as how to communicate data-driven insights to stakeholders.
  • Applied Algebra - C957
    This class is designed to help students develop their understanding of mathematical concepts, such as algebra and functions. Students will learn how to solve equations and inequalities, graph linear and quadratic equations, and work with polynomials. They will also learn how to use algebraic concepts in real-world applications.
  • Ethics in Technology - D333
    This class explores ethical issues related to technology, including issues related to privacy, security, and intellectual property. It covers topics such as ethical decision-making in the context of technology, and how to apply ethical principles to technology-related issues.
  • Emerging Technologies in Cybersecurity - C844
    The course emphasizes the need for cybersecurity professionals to analyze and evaluate new technologies, particularly wireless, mobile, and internet technologies, to address the security requirements of an organization and teaches the underlying principles critical to the operation of secure networks and the adoption of new technologies.
  • Digital Forensics in Cybersecurity - D431
    Covers the principles and practices of digital forensics, which is the process of collecting, analyzing, and preserving digital evidence in order to investigate and prevent cybercrime. Students will learn about the tools and techniques used in digital forensics, as well as the legal and ethical considerations involved in conducting digital forensic investigations. They will also learn about the various types of cybercrime and the methods used to detect and prevent it.

Incomplete

  • Information Systems Security - C845
    This class is designed to help students understand the principles of information security and how to protect data, networks and systems from unauthorized access and malicious attacks. Students will learn about the different types of threats and vulnerabilities and how to implement security controls and incident response procedures to protect against them.
  • Managing Cloud Security - C838
    This class is designed to help students understand the security challenges of cloud computing and the best practices for securing cloud environments. Students will learn about the different types of cloud services and their security features, as well as how to implement security controls to protect data and applications in the cloud.
  • Introduction to Communication - D268
    This class is designed to help students develop their communication skills. Students will learn about different types of communication, such as verbal, nonverbal, and written communication. They will also learn about the different types of communication styles, such as active and passive communication, and how to communicate effectively in different settings.
  • Managing Information Security - C843
    This course teaches students how to develop and manage an information security program that aligns with business goals, complies with regulations, minimizes risk, and delivers value to enterprises.
  • Cyber Defense and Countermeasures - D340
    This course teaches students how to respond to computer security incidents by covering principles and techniques for detecting, preventing, and recovering from threats, and prepares students for the CompTIA Cybersecurity Analyst (CySA+) certification exam, with prerequisites being Networks and Network and Security - Applications.
  • Linux Foundations - D281
    Linux Foundations prepares learners for the LPI Linux Essentials certification, and is an introduction to Linux as an operating system as well as an introduction to open-source concepts and the basics of the Linux command line.
  • Penetration Testing and Vulnerability Analysis - D332
    The course teaches learners how to perform penetration testing and vulnerability management within an organization by developing skills in planning, cyber reconnaissance, exploitation of vulnerabilities, simulating attacks and responses, and reporting results with recommended actions.
  • IT Capstone Written Project - C769
    The capstone project involves proposing and implementing a technical work plan, followed by a post-implementation report, which should be presented and approved by the course instructor in line with the graduate's technical specialization.
